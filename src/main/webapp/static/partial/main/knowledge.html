Knowledge base

Basic attributes

  • Movement point (MVP)

    How many things can a character do under a turn. It's used up for every movement. A new human character's movement points is 30 and it regenerates 1 points / turn.

Combat attributes

  • Life points (LP)

    Life points shows the amount of damage a character can receive before dying. If the character's LP reach 0 then it dies. The base life point is 15 + half of the vitality and endurance attributes. A new human character's life points is 30 and it regenerates 3 points / turn.

  • Mana points (MP)

    Mana points are used for spellcasting. A starting character's MP is 0 + half of the intelligence, wisdom and willpower attributes. A new human character's mana points is 30 and it regenerates 3 points / turn.

  • Initiation

    The initiation attribute determines who attack first in combat. The starting initiation is 10 + half of the swiftness and perception attributes. A new human character's initiation points is 20.

  • Attack

    The attack attribute determines the chance of damaging the enemy in meele combat. The starting attack is 10 + half of the dexterity and swiftness. A new human character's attack points is 20.

  • Aiming

    The attack attribute determines the chance of damaging the enemy in ranged combat. The starting aiming is 10 + half of the dexterity and perception. A new human character's aiming points is 20.

  • Defense

    The defense attribute determines the chance of avoiding the opponents attacks in combat. The starting defense is 10 + half of the endurance and dexterity. A new human character's defense points is 20.

  • Spell resistance

    The spell resistance attribute determines the chance of avoiding or decreasing the opponents magical attacks or damage in combat. The starting spell resistance is 10 + half of the willpower and intelligence. A new human character's spell resistance points is 20.

  • Damage

    The damage attribute determines the amount of casualty the character can deal to it's opponent in melee combat. The starting damage is 1 + the quarter of the strength attribute. A new human character's damage is 3.

  • Ranged damage

    The damage attribute determines the amount of casualty the character can deal to it's opponent in ranged combat. The starting ranged damage is 1 + the quarter of the dexterity attribute. A new human character's damage is 3.
